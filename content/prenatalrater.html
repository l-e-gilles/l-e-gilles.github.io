App Logo Design
One of the last things that needed designing was the app icon. Many ideas were discussed, the icon could have included a graphic of a baby or mother, but the team thought a supplement or pill bottle would better fit the app's purpose.

I presented multiple designs, so it would be easier for the sponsor/team to narrow down a final design choice.

The first interation, main differences is the green or blue being the primary color. It lacked any indicators of what the app does.
The second iteration had question marks to imply "Which supplement should I choose?". However, the team did not like this design.
For the third iteration, I removed one of the pills and rotated the other to reduce the busyness. The team still didn't like the question mark, and instead we changed it to the percentage symbol.
Fourth iteration was testing out different % sizes. The larger one coverd up too much of the pill, so it was placed inside the pill. The team decided on the green background.
Iteration five and the final design, the sponsor wanted "NHF" visible in the icon so the intials were added.
❮ ❯
Results
The app was than coded and published, with some design changes. The sponsor wanted the home page background color changed to white. Additional graphics were added.

The iOS had the most design changes. Instead of a drop down navigation menu, a bottom navbar was used instead. The supplement filter page utilized slider bars similar to Android's rather than using chips.

Overall, the final product looked and functioned as I originally designed.
